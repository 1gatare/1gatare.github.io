Peacebuilding and Psychosocial Support
Overview

Peace building and psychosocial support is one of the most impacting initiatives that Prison Fellowship Rwanda has introduced since its inception. Community-based initiatives related to mental health and social cohesion at grassroots level are implemented in communities and in prisons to resolve conflicts, mitigate crimes, promote practical reconciliation, social cohesion and to contribute to the efforts towards sustainable peace in Rwanda. PFR is implementing following projects under Peacebuilding and psychosocial support.

1.Societal Healing Program

In partnership with the Rwandan Government’s Ministry of National Unity and Civic Engagement (MINUBUMWE), Interpeace, Haguruka, Dignity in Detention (DIDE), and the Embassy of Sweden as a donor, Prison Fellowship Rwanda (PFR) is implementing the program Reinforcing Community Capacity for Social Cohesion and Reconciliation through Societal Trauma Healing' in five districts: Nyagatare, Ngoma, Musanze, Nyabihu, and Nyamagabe. The societal healing program adopts a comprehensive and distinctive approach, providing mental health treatments, improving social cohesion, and developing long-term livelihoods for the intended beneficiaries. These include Genocide survivors, Genocide perpetrators, ex-combatants, and returnees from 1959 to 1994 (current and former/released offenders), in addition to the families of these individuals, youth, and local leaders. Other planned participants are community-based groups and entities involved in mental health, social cohesion, and joint economic development initiatives at the community level. The programme provides safe spaces for dialogue, healing, and capacity strengthening of existing formal mental health structures and local societal healing practitioners to strengthen a comprehensive and community-based mental health and psychosocial support system. Our program aims to contribute to the consolidation of a peaceful, resilient, and inclusive Rwandan society, enabling it to overcome the wounds of the past, peacefully manage conflicts and diversity, collectively and inclusively strengthen livelihood resilience, and empower citizens to influence policies responsive to their priorities. It is implemented through three axes: mental health, social cohesion, and sustainable collaborative livelihoods. Gender, climate resilience, and conflict sensitivity aspects are also integrated as cross-cutting issues across the three axes.

2.Advancing Human Right in Rwanda

Prison Fellowship Rwanda, in partnership with the Rwanda Bridge to Justice (RBJ), Initiatives for Peace and Human Rights (IPeace), and International Bridge to Justice (IBJ), is implementing a project titled "Advancing Human Rights in Rwanda" with the goal of expanding holistic legal and rehabilitative protection to communities at risk of torture, arbitrary detention, and other forms of abuse. The role of Prison Fellowship Rwanda in this project is offering psychosocial support to inmates.

Restorative Justice
Overview

Our Restorative Justice program represents a transformative approach to justice that emphasizes healing, accountability, and the restoration of relationships damaged by crime and conflict.

Program Elements
  • Victim-Offender Dialogue: Facilitated meetings between victims and offenders to promote understanding and healing.
  • Community Conferences: Bringing together all affected parties to address harm and determine appropriate restitution.
  • Reintegration Support: Programs helping offenders successfully return to their communities.
  • Reconciliation Workshops: Group sessions focusing on forgiveness and relationship rebuilding.
Outcomes

Our restorative justice initiatives have shown significant success in reducing recidivism, increasing victim satisfaction with the justice process, and strengthening community bonds.

Human Rights Promotion & Legal Aid
Overview

Our Human Rights Promotion & Legal Aid program ensures access to justice and protects the fundamental rights of all individuals, particularly those affected by the criminal justice system.

Services Provided
  • Legal Representation: Pro bono legal services for those who cannot afford representation.
  • Rights Education: Workshops and training sessions on human rights and legal literacy.
  • Advocacy: Working with authorities to improve conditions and protect rights.
  • Case Management: Comprehensive support throughout legal proceedings.
Success Stories

Through this program, we have successfully advocated for improved prison conditions, secured fair trials for numerous individuals, and helped implement policies protecting prisoners' rights.

Spiritual Resilience
Overview

Our Spiritual Resilience program focuses on nurturing the spiritual well-being of individuals, helping them find hope, purpose, and inner strength through faith-based support.

Program Activities
  • Spiritual Counseling: One-on-one pastoral care and guidance.
  • Prayer Groups: Regular gatherings for spiritual support and community building.
  • Faith Studies: Educational programs exploring spiritual growth and understanding.
  • Worship Services: Regular opportunities for communal worship and reflection.
Transformation Stories

Many participants have experienced profound personal transformation through this program, finding renewed purpose, hope, and strength to overcome life's challenges.
